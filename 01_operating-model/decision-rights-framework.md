# Decision Rights Framework

## Purpose
This framework defines who has authority to decide, who provides input, and where escalation is required.

## Decision-rights model

| Area | Decision owner | Required input | Escalate when |
|---|---|---|---|
| Domain ownership | Governance lead / executive sponsor | Business leadership | Multiple units claim or reject ownership |
| Business glossary term approval | Data owner | Steward, SME | Cross-functional disagreement persists |
| Data quality threshold approval | Data owner | Steward, analyst, reporting lead | Threshold breach has enterprise impact |
| Access to sensitive data | Data owner / delegated approver | Privacy, security, steward | Request is exceptional or high risk |
| Exception approval | Governance council or named authority | Owner, steward, control owner | Risk exceeds delegated authority |
| Change to key calculation logic | Data owner | Steward, analyst, impacted users | Downstream reporting or compliance risk is material |
| Issue priority assignment | Data steward | Owner, analyst, operational lead | Issue affects multiple domains or executive reporting |

## Decision principles
- authority must be explicit
- approvals must be traceable
- exceptions must be documented
- decisions affecting multiple domains require broader review
- urgency does not remove accountability

## Delegation
Decision authority can be delegated only when:
- the delegation is documented
- the scope of delegation is clear
- exceptions and expiry conditions are defined
- high-risk items remain escalatable

## Evidence requirements
High-impact decisions should reference:
- affected asset or domain
- rationale
- risk level
- consulted parties
- effective date
- review date where applicable
