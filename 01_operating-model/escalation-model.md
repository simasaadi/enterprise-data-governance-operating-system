# Escalation Model

## Purpose
This model defines when issues move beyond routine stewardship handling and require formal management or governance review.

## Escalation triggers
Escalation should occur when:
- no owner can be confirmed
- a data quality issue remains unresolved past target date
- multiple teams disagree on business meaning or use
- a control failure affects executive reporting or operations
- a requested access decision falls outside standard rules
- a change has material downstream impact
- repeated exceptions indicate a structural weakness

## Escalation levels

| Level | Trigger | Owner | Expected action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Routine issue or clarification | Data steward | Triage, assign, track |
| Level 2 | Cross-team issue or repeated control failure | Data owner / manager | Resolve ownership, approve action |
| Level 3 | Material risk or unresolved conflict | Governance lead / council | Decision, direction, risk acceptance or remediation mandate |
| Level 4 | Enterprise or executive risk | Executive sponsor | Strategic decision and oversight |

## Escalation requirements
Each escalation should capture:
- issue summary
- affected asset or report
- business impact
- control impact
- requested decision
- due date
- accountable owner
- current status

## Escalation discipline
Escalation is not failure. It is a control mechanism that prevents ambiguity, delay, and unmanaged risk.
