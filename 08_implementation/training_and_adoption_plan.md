# Training and Adoption Plan

## Purpose
This plan defines how users, stewards, owners, and managers will be enabled to use the governance operating system in practice.

## Objective
The objective is to prevent governance from remaining a document set that exists on paper but does not change operating behavior.

## Adoption principles
- train people on tasks they actually perform
- tie training to role-specific responsibilities
- use examples from real workflows and assets
- reinforce why the governance change matters
- make adoption measurable
- treat communication, training, and reinforcement as part of implementation

## Key audiences

| Audience | What they need |
|---|---|
| Data Owners | Decision rights, thresholds, access approval, escalation responsibility |
| Data Stewards | Metadata maintenance, issue triage, workflow coordination, KPI reporting |
| Analysts / SMEs | Control execution, issue evidence, change impact input, dictionary use |
| Managers | How governance affects reporting confidence, access review, and operational risk |
| System Owners / Admins | How technical enablement aligns with business approval and control expectations |
| Executive Sponsors | Progress, risks, maturity, and management action required |

## Training modules

### Module 1: Governance operating model
Audience:
- all core governance participants

Covers:
- governance structure
- roles and responsibilities
- decision rights
- escalation model
- expected forums and cadence

### Module 2: Data quality and issue handling
Audience:
- stewards, analysts, owners, managers

Covers:
- control types
- thresholds
- issue intake
- remediation and validation
- recurrence and root cause review

### Module 3: Metadata and definition discipline
Audience:
- stewards, analysts, owners

Covers:
- glossary use
- dictionary maintenance
- approved definitions
- metadata minimums
- impact analysis linkage

### Module 4: Access governance
Audience:
- owners, stewards, managers, admins

Covers:
- classification model
- minimum necessary access
- access request workflow
- exception logging
- review expectations

### Module 5: Change governance and communication
Audience:
- stewards, analysts, managers, owners

Covers:
- when change impact assessment is required
- how to issue change notices
- how to validate downstream impacts
- how to avoid unmanaged change risk

## Training delivery methods

| Method | Best use |
|---|---|
| Live working session | Role clarity and workflow walkthrough |
| Job aid / quick guide | Repeatable day-to-day reference |
| Recorded demo | Tool or workbook walkthrough |
| Office hours | Adoption support after launch |
| Manager briefing | Reinforce accountability and expectations |

## Adoption supports
Strong adoption usually needs:
- role-based quick guides
- examples populated with realistic cases
- simple intake and decision templates
- recurring check-ins
- manager reinforcement
- visible KPI follow-up

## Adoption measures
Use measures such as:
- percent of priority roles trained
- percent of issue cases logged versus handled informally
- percent of material access decisions documented
- percent of material changes assessed before implementation
- stewardship forum attendance and action completion
- growth in metadata and glossary coverage
- reduction in repeat issues

## Common adoption barriers
- generic training not tied to role
- unclear authority
- unclear benefit to operational teams
- artifact overload
- weak manager reinforcement
- limited time for stewards and analysts
- governance introduced without workflow integration

## Reinforcement plan
After initial launch:
- hold office hours for the first 4 to 8 weeks
- review early issues and decision logs with users
- spotlight common mistakes and clarifications
- publish a short monthly governance update
- refresh training where repeat confusion appears

## Governance rule
Training is not complete when materials are delivered. It is complete when governance behaviors are visible in practice.
