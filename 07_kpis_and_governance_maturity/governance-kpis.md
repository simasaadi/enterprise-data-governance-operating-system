# Governance KPIs

## Purpose
This document defines the key performance indicators used to monitor whether the governance operating system is functioning effectively across ownership, quality, metadata, access, issue handling, and change management.

## Objective
The objective is to move governance from a documentation exercise to a measurable operating capability with visible evidence of progress, risk, and control performance.

## KPI design principles
- KPIs should measure operating behavior, not only activity volume
- metrics should support management action, not vanity reporting
- indicators should be traceable to governance objectives
- a balance of effectiveness, timeliness, and risk indicators is needed
- recurring failures should be visible, not hidden inside averages

## KPI categories

| Category | What it measures |
|---|---|
| Ownership and stewardship | Whether accountability is assigned and active |
| Data quality | Whether controls are executed and thresholds are met |
| Metadata and definitions | Whether governed assets are documented and reviewed |
| Access governance | Whether access is controlled, reviewed, and traceable |
| Issue and remediation | Whether problems are resolved in a timely, durable way |
| Change governance | Whether material changes are assessed and communicated |
| Adoption and maturity | Whether governance is becoming embedded in practice |

## Core KPI set

### 1. Governed asset ownership coverage
**Definition:** Percent of critical governed assets with a named data owner and steward.  
**Formula:** governed assets with owner and steward / total critical governed assets  
**Target direction:** Higher is better

### 2. Metadata coverage rate
**Definition:** Percent of critical governed assets with minimum required metadata completed.  
**Formula:** critical assets meeting metadata minimum / total critical assets  
**Target direction:** Higher is better

### 3. Approved glossary coverage
**Definition:** Percent of critical business terms used in shared reporting that have approved definitions.  
**Formula:** approved critical terms / total critical reporting terms  
**Target direction:** Higher is better

### 4. Control execution rate
**Definition:** Percent of scheduled governance or data quality controls executed on time.  
**Formula:** controls executed on time / controls scheduled  
**Target direction:** Higher is better

### 5. Threshold breach rate
**Definition:** Percent of executed controls that breached approved thresholds.  
**Formula:** breached controls / executed controls  
**Target direction:** Lower is better

### 6. High-risk open issue count
**Definition:** Number of unresolved high-risk or critical governance issues.  
**Formula:** count of open high-risk and critical issues  
**Target direction:** Lower is better

### 7. On-time issue resolution rate
**Definition:** Percent of issues resolved by target resolution date.  
**Formula:** issues resolved on time / total issues resolved  
**Target direction:** Higher is better

### 8. Repeat issue recurrence rate
**Definition:** Percent of closed issues that recur within the defined monitoring window.  
**Formula:** recurring issues / closed issues  
**Target direction:** Lower is better

### 9. Traceable access decision rate
**Definition:** Percent of material access decisions documented in the decision log.  
**Formula:** logged material access decisions / total material access decisions  
**Target direction:** Higher is better

### 10. Temporary access expiry compliance
**Definition:** Percent of temporary access grants reviewed or removed by expiry date.  
**Formula:** temporary access cases closed or reviewed on time / total temporary access cases  
**Target direction:** Higher is better

### 11. Change assessment completion rate
**Definition:** Percent of material changes completed with documented impact assessment before implementation.  
**Formula:** material changes with completed assessment / total material changes  
**Target direction:** Higher is better

### 12. Change notice timeliness
**Definition:** Percent of material stakeholder-affecting changes communicated before effective date.  
**Formula:** material changes noticed on time / total material stakeholder-affecting changes  
**Target direction:** Higher is better

## KPI interpretation guidance
Strong performance usually shows:
- high ownership and metadata coverage
- high control execution
- low repeat issues
- strong traceability for access and change decisions
- improving timeliness and fewer unresolved high-risk issues

Weak performance often shows:
- low ownership coverage
- controls executed inconsistently
- repeated threshold breaches
- open issues aging without resolution
- undocumented exceptions
- changes implemented without stakeholder visibility

## Minimum governance reporting view
A monthly governance KPI view should ideally show:
- current month result
- prior month result
- target or threshold
- status indicator
- key commentary
- owner for follow-up

## Governance rule
KPIs should not be reported without:
- a clear definition
- a known owner
- a data source
- a review cadence
- a response expectation when performance declines
