# Governance Maturity Model

## Purpose
This model defines how governance capability can be assessed over time across core operating dimensions.

## Objective
The objective is to help leaders understand whether governance is informal, emerging, repeatable, controlled, or well-embedded and to identify the next practical improvement steps.

## Maturity scale

| Level | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ad Hoc | Governance depends on individuals, memory, and informal workarounds |
| 2 | Emerging | Some roles, templates, or controls exist, but usage is inconsistent |
| 3 | Repeatable | Core governance processes are defined and used with reasonable consistency |
| 4 | Controlled | Governance is measured, monitored, and tied to accountabilities and review cycles |
| 5 | Embedded | Governance is integrated into delivery, change, reporting, and management decision-making |

## Assessment dimensions

| Dimension | What is assessed |
|---|---|
| Operating model | Clarity of structure, forums, and escalation pathways |
| Roles and stewardship | Ownership clarity, stewarding discipline, role execution |
| Data quality controls | Defined controls, thresholds, monitoring, and remediation |
| Metadata and definitions | Coverage, approval, update discipline, and usability |
| Access governance | Classification, approval pathways, traceability, minimum-necessary design |
| Issue and change management | Structured intake, root cause review, impact assessment, communication |
| KPI monitoring | Whether governance performance is measured and reviewed |
| Adoption and implementation | Whether governance is used in practice beyond documentation |

## Maturity characteristics by level

### Level 1: Ad Hoc
- roles unclear or person-dependent
- issues handled through email and memory
- definitions differ across teams
- access decisions are informal
- controls are limited or inconsistent
- little or no governance measurement

### Level 2: Emerging
- some templates or roles exist
- governance activity depends on motivated individuals
- high-risk assets may be partially documented
- issue logging begins but is incomplete
- access decisions are somewhat structured
- limited leadership visibility

### Level 3: Repeatable
- core processes are defined and reused
- owners and stewards are generally identifiable
- issue intake and escalation are structured
- metadata exists for many critical assets
- access decisions are more traceable
- control execution and KPI review occur on a regular cadence

### Level 4: Controlled
- governance KPIs are actively monitored
- repeat issues trigger structural remediation
- control failures and access exceptions are visible
- change assessment is consistently required
- metadata and glossary records are maintained with review discipline
- governance forums influence decisions

### Level 5: Embedded
- governance is integrated into operating and delivery processes
- decisions, changes, and access reviews consistently follow governance pathways
- leaders rely on governance metrics and maturity results
- controls are designed into workflows rather than added after issues
- governance is sustainable beyond individual champions

## Example assessment grid

| Dimension | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 | Level 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Operating model | Informal | Partial structure | Defined structure | Reviewed and managed | Fully integrated |
| Ownership | Unclear | Partially assigned | Mostly assigned | Measured and enforced | Embedded in delivery |
| Data quality | Reactive | Some checks | Defined controls | Measured and escalated | Preventive and continuous |
| Metadata | Sparse | Partial | Broad coverage | Reviewed and maintained | Integrated into workflow |
| Access governance | Informal | Basic approval | Structured approval | Traceable and reviewed | Risk-based and embedded |
| Issue management | Ad hoc | Logged inconsistently | Structured triage | Trend-managed | Preventive learning loop |
| Change governance | Minimal | Case-by-case | Defined assessment | Consistently applied | Integrated and proactive |
| KPI monitoring | None | Limited | Regular reporting | Active management | Strategic oversight |

## Suggested scoring approach
Each dimension can be scored from 1 to 5 based on observed practice.

### Example method
- 1.0 to 1.9 = Ad Hoc
- 2.0 to 2.9 = Emerging
- 3.0 to 3.9 = Repeatable
- 4.0 to 4.9 = Controlled
- 5.0 = Embedded

## Evidence sources for maturity scoring
Use evidence such as:
- governance artifacts
- issue and access logs
- control execution evidence
- metadata records
- stakeholder interviews
- meeting outputs
- change assessments
- KPI dashboards

## Maturity improvement logic
The goal is not to maximize policy volume. The goal is to move from:
- ambiguity to clarity
- person-dependence to operating discipline
- reactive correction to structured prevention
- isolated artifacts to connected governance workflow

## Governance rule
A maturity score should only be assigned when it is supported by observable evidence rather than intention alone.
